HTML entities - &xxxx;

Used in the place of reserved characters and difficult to type characters.

Using reserved characters in a text may cause troubles like using <.

Like HTML reserved characters: <,>,&, etc.

Difficult to type, like symbols: ♠, ♥, ⩇

Always start with ampersand and end with semicolon. &something;


Can be: &entity_name; OR &#entity_number;

non-breaking space

non-breaking space: &nbsp; &#160; is very important.

A non-breaking space is a space that will not break into a new line.

Two words separated by a non-breaking space will stick together (not break into a new line). This is handy when breaking the words might be disruptive.

Examples:

  • ¬ß 10
  • 10 km/h
  • 10 PM
Another common use of the non-breaking space is to prevent browsers from truncating spaces in HTML pages.

If you write 10 spaces in your text, the browser will remove 9 of them. To add real spaces to your text, you can use the   character entity.
